Preparing Your Home

Flooding and severe storms could heavily impact your home and safety if you are not prepared. Ensuring that a couple of preparations are made could lead to the safety of your home and decreasing the amount of damage done. Home Preparations

A great way to ensure that your home is safe and prepared is to ensure the quality of your pipes and drainage systems. Doing so will help prevent the risks of leaks and indoor flooding. Another precaution is to use sandbags, although this method is typically only used for when evacuation is not necessary, but water might reach your home. Sandbags should be around a third to half full and be placed in stacks. Use them to block drainage, doors, and other low lying areas where water can potentially seep through. It should be noted that sandbags are not a foolproof method for stopping water flow, and should be used in conjunction with other methods.

Basic Disaster Supplies Kit

  • Emergency Supply List
  • Water (1 gallon per person, per day for drinking and sanitation, accounting for several days)
  • Food (at least a several-day supply of non-perishable food)
  • Battery-powered or hand crank radio and a NOAA Weather Radio with tone alert
  • Flashlight
  • First aid kit
  • Extra batteries
  • Whistle (to signal for help)
  • Dust mask (to help filter contaminated air)
  • Plastic sheeting and duct tape (to shelter in place)
  • Moist towelettes, garbage bags and plastic ties (for personal sanitation)
  • Wrench or pliers (to turn off utilities)
  • Manual can opener (for food)
  • Local maps
  • Cell phone with chargers and a backup battery
